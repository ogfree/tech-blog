## Team Analysis: Duke Blue Devils (2025-26 Season)

**Record & Performance:** Duke enters this game with a 14-3 record, ranked #7 in the AP Poll. Their non-conference slate was brutal, featuring wins over Kansas and a narrow loss to Gonzaga. They're averaging 78.5 points per game while holding opponents to just 62.1, a testament to their balanced attack.

**Key Players to Watch:**
*   **Tyrese Proctor (G):** The Australian guard has taken the leap into stardom. Averaging 18.2 points and 5.8 assists per game, he's the engine of Duke's offense. His ability to control tempo and hit clutch shots will be critical.
*   **Kyle Filipowski (C):** The 7-foot center is a matchup nightmare. With 16.5 points and 10.1 rebounds per game, he dominates the paint. California's frontcourt will have their hands full containing his post moves and mid-range jumper.
*   **Cooper Flagg (F):** The sophomore is a defensive force, averaging 2.3 blocks and 1.8 steals. His versatility on both ends makes him a potential game-changer.

**Strengths & Weaknesses:** Duke's strength is their defensive identity and transition offense. They force turnovers and convert them into easy points. However, they can be vulnerable to physical, grind-it-out teams that limit their pace. Their three-point shooting can also go cold, making them reliant on Proctor's drives and Filipowski's interior scoring.

## Team Analysis: California Golden Bears (2025-26 Season)

**Record & Performance:** California is 13-4, ranked #21 in the nation. They've built their resume with a tough schedule, including a win over a ranked USC team. They score 75.3 points per game but give up 68.9, indicating a more offensive-minded approach. Their home court advantage in Berkeley is real—the Bears are 10-1 at Haas Pavilion this season.

**Key Players to Watch:**
*   **Jalen Blackmon (G):** The transfer from Stephen F. Austin is a walking bucket. Leading the team with 21.4 points per game, he's a lethal scorer from all three levels. Duke's perimeter defense will need to be locked in.
*   **Fardaws Aimaq (F/C):** The veteran big man is a double-double machine (14.8 PPG, 11.2 RPG). His physicality and rebounding could disrupt Duke's rhythm. He’s also a capable passer from the high post.
*   **Jermaine Terry (G):** The sophomore guard is the glue guy. Averaging 9.5 points and 4.2 assists, he’s the primary ball-handler and a tenacious defender.

**Strengths & Weaknesses:** California's strength is their offensive firepower and rebounding. They crash the boards hard and have multiple scoring options. Their weakness is defensive consistency, especially on the perimeter. They can be prone to giving up open looks if their help defense isn't sharp, which is dangerous against a team like Duke.
